Motion Principles

Firefox motion is quick, supportive and whimsical.

Story

Quick, supportive, and whimsical are the three principles that guide the motion design language of Firefox. Following these principles, we aim to achieve a cohesive, enjoyable, and consistent experience within the family of Firefox browsers.

Quick

Animations should be fast and nimble and never keep the user waiting longer than they need to. The aim is to prioritise user perceived performance over over technical benchmarks.

Whimsical

Animations should have a playful and Delightful personality when appropriate.

Supportive

Motion should help ease the user through the experience. It should aid the flow of actions, giving clear guidance for user orientation: spatial or temporal.

Curves

A rigidly linear movement looks unnatural to the human eye. Accelerate and decelerate smoothly helps your animation to feel more natural.

Photon curve

In Firefox we use this curve for the majority of animations. Acceleration and deceleration occur asymmetrically to feel natural.

We quickly accelerate at the beginning of the animation and slowly decelerate by the end.

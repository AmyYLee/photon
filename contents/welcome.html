Intro

Firefox offers users a great deal of control over things like privacy, security, and customization.  This means that the user experience can be highly personal and idiosyncratic. Our products need to support this, but we still want them to look, feel and sound like they come from the same place and share the same spirit.

That’s why we’ve created these guidelines. Having a consistent appearance and voice builds credibility and trust for people who use Firefox. Having well-articulated values and well-documented standards also makes decisions easier for people who design for Firefox.

Whether you are building an add-on, designing a new feature or writing support copy, we hope these guidelines help you use Firefox design principles and systems in your own work.

Goal

The goal we set for the design system name goes here is to create a modern and faster experience. Our designs should be unified platforms that drive greater efficiency through well-defined and reusable components.

Principles

We established a few principles to guide us during the design process. These principles help us to define our visual languange.

Adaptable

Firefox should adapt itself for everyone: From new users to seasoned Mozillians. Customization and control of your web experience is key.

Approachable

Firefox should feel welcoming and friendly.

Aware

Firefox should be perceptive of you and your needs, while remaining respectful of your personal/private space.

Quick

Firefox should feel fast! From task completion to icon animation.

Supportive

Firefox should be helpful in both proactive and passive ways.

Whimsical

Firefox should be fun to use! Our browser should delight the user in both expected and unexpected ways.
